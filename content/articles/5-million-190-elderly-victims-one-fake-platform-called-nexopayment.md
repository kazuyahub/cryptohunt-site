<h2>The Investigation That Exposed NEXOpayment</h2><figure class="w-richtext-figure-type-image w-richtext-align-normal" data-rt-type="image" data-rt-align="normal"><div><img alt="__wf_reserved_inherit" src="https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/69c6e47d43da620ce27fc19e/6a2293fc21904c5f2da50375_Nexo-expose.png" loading="lazy"></div></figure><p>In February 2026, NSW Police established Strike Force Resaca to investigate one of Australia's largest cryptocurrency investment scams in recent years.</p><p>The focus of the investigation was a platform known as NEXOpayment.</p><p>According to investigators, more than 190 Australians lost over $5 million after depositing funds into what appeared to be a legitimate cryptocurrency and investment platform.</p><p>Most victims were elderly Australians who believed they were investing through trusted financial advisors.</p><h2>How The NEXOpayment Scam Worked</h2><p>The operation followed a familiar pattern seen across many modern cryptocurrency investment scams.</p><p>Victims were approached through social media, phone calls, and online messaging platforms by individuals presenting themselves as investment experts.</p><p>The scammers directed victims to NEXOpayment, a professional looking platform that claimed to offer cryptocurrency trading, stock investments, and wealth building opportunities.</p><p>Users could log into their accounts and view balances that appeared to grow over time.</p><p>Everything seemed legitimate.</p><p>According to NSW Police, the displayed profits were an illusion designed to encourage larger deposits.</p><p>When victims transferred funds, the money was allegedly routed through multiple cryptocurrency wallets and exchanges rather than being invested.</p><h2>Strike Force Resaca Findings</h2><p>Investigators tracked suspicious financial movements connected to the platform and identified a network of transactions consistent with money laundering activity.</p><p>During searches conducted at properties in Strathfield and Cammeray, police seized electronic devices and documentation linked to the investigation.</p><p>A 42 year old man was arrested and charged with dealing in proceeds of crime.</p><p>The investigation remains a significant example of how organised cybercriminal operations exploit cryptocurrency infrastructure to move stolen funds.</p><h2>Why Older Australians Were Targeted</h2><p>Investment scams continue to disproportionately affect older Australians.</p><p>Retirees and individuals approaching retirement often possess larger savings balances and may actively seek investment opportunities that generate passive income.</p><p>Criminal groups understand this and frequently tailor their marketing toward older demographics.</p><p>The scammers behind NEXOpayment reportedly used professional language, polished websites, and convincing investment narratives to build trust before requesting deposits.</p><h2>Following The Money</h2><p>One of the most important discoveries in the investigation was the movement of funds after victims deposited money into the platform.</p><p>Instead of remaining within investment accounts, funds were transferred across multiple cryptocurrency wallets and exchanges.</p><p>This process is known as layering and is commonly used to obscure the origin of funds.</p><p>Once assets move through several wallets and platforms, recovering stolen money becomes significantly more difficult.</p><p>This is one reason cryptocurrency investment scams remain highly profitable for organised criminal groups.</p><h2>Key Warning Signs Identified</h2><p>The NEXOpayment case highlights several warning signs that investors should always watch for:</p><ul><li>Unsolicited investment opportunities</li><li>Contact through social media or messaging apps</li><li>Promises of strong or consistent returns</li><li>Platforms with little public reputation</li><li>Difficulty withdrawing funds</li><li>Advisors pushing urgent investment decisions</li><li>Requests to deposit additional funds before withdrawals are processed</li></ul><h2>Lessons From The Investigation</h2><p>The collapse of NEXOpayment demonstrates how convincing fraudulent investment platforms have become.</p><p>Professional design, fake account balances, and persuasive advisors can create the appearance of legitimacy even when no real investments exist.</p><p>Before depositing funds into any cryptocurrency platform:</p><ul><li>Verify AUSTRAC registration</li><li>Search ASIC MoneySmart scam warnings</li><li>Research the company independently</li><li>Verify team members and business registrations</li><li>Test withdrawals before committing larger amounts</li></ul><p>A few minutes of verification can prevent devastating financial losses.</p><h2>Conclusion</h2><p>The NEXOpayment investigation serves as a reminder that investment scams remain one of the fastest growing forms of cybercrime in Australia.</p><p>More than 190 victims lost over $5 million in just a few months before authorities intervened.</p><p>As cryptocurrency adoption continues to grow, investors should remain cautious of unsolicited opportunities and unknown trading platforms.</p><p>Trust should never replace verification.</p><h2>Sources</h2><ul><li>NSW
