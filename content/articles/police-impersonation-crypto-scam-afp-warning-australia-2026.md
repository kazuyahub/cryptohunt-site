<h2>Fake AFP Officers Are Calling Australians to Steal Their Crypto</h2><p>A police impersonation crypto scam is sweeping across Australia. Criminals phone Australians while posing as AFP officers, then walk-them toward handing over their crypto, wallet access, or seed phrase.</p><h2>How the Scam Works</ol><ol><li>A call from someone claiming to be AFP, often on a spoofed number.</li><li>They say your details appeared in a data breach linked to crypto fraud.</li><li>They provide a ReportCyber reference number.</li><li>A "second caller" from your exchange confirms the same number.</li><li>You are pressured to move funds to a "safe" account controlled by scammers.</li></ol><h2>Warning Signs</h2><p>Any unsolicited AFP call, ReportCyber reference you didn't create, requests for seed phrases or passwords, or pressure to move funds.</p><h2>How To Protect Yourself</h2><p>Stop and think. Legitimate police will never ask for your wallet details or pressure you to move funds. Hang up and call the organisation back on a number you find yourself.</p>
