<h2>Why Fake Crypto Exchanges Are So Dangerous</h2><figure class="w-richtext-figure-type-image w-richtext-align-normal" data-rt-type="image" data-rt-align="normal" data-rt-max-width=""><div><img alt="__wf_reserved_inherit" src="https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/69c6e47d43da620ce27fc19e/6a228eaecbd3ee4d257df574_blog-3-sec.webp" width="auto" height="auto" loading="lazy"></div></figure><p>Fake cryptocurrency exchanges continue to target Australian investors through social media, messaging apps and online advertisements.</p><p>Many scam platforms look professional. They often include:</p><ul><li>Modern website designs</li><li>Live customer support</li><li>Trading dashboards</li><li>Fake user reviews</li><li>Simulated profits</li></ul><p>The goal is simple: convince users to deposit funds before restricting withdrawals or disappearing entirely.</p><p>Understanding how to verify an exchange before investing can prevent significant financial losses.</p><h2>Step 1: Check AUSTRAC Registration</h2><p>Any cryptocurrency exchange operating legally in Australia must be registered with AUSTRAC.</p><p>Before depositing funds</p><ul><li>Visit the AUSTRAC website</li><li>Search the Reporting Entities Register</li><li>Verify the exchange independently</li></ul><p>If the platform cannot be found, treat it as a major warning sign.</p><h2>Step 2: Search Scam Reports</h2><p>Before using any exchange:</p><ul><li>Search the platform name on Google</li><li>Search the platform name with the word scam</li><li>Review ASIC MoneySmart scam alerts</li><li>Check crypto forums</li></ul><h2>Step 3: Inspect The Website Domain</h2><p>Pay attention to domain extensions, misspelled names, and domain age before trusting any platform.</p><h2>Step 4: Verify The Team</h2><p>Legitimate exchanges are transparent about leadership. Reverse image searches can often reveal stolen profile pictures.</p><h2>Step 5: Test Withdrawals Early</h2><p>Many scam exchanges allow deposits but create obstacles when users attempt to withdraw. Legitimate exchanges do not require additional deposits to release your own funds.</p><h2>Common Red Flags</h2><p>Unregistered exchange, guaranteed profits, withdrawal restrictions, anonymous operators, social media introductions, and Telegram-only support.</p><h2>Final Thoughts</h2><p>A few minutes of due diligence can prevent significant financial losses.</p>
